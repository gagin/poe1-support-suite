## 2. Delve Depth for XP at Level 97.5

**Current depth 267 is already optimal.** The problem is structural, not depth-related.

- Delve monster level **caps at 83** around depth 250-270. Going deeper does NOT raise monster level — only makes mobs tankier for zero extra XP.
- At level 97.5, XP penalty against level 83 monsters is ~93% — you receive roughly **7% of base XP** per kill. This is identical to running T16 maps.
- Going shallower (200-250) may be better if you can clear faster there — mobs are still level 83 but with less bonus life/damage.

### Recommendations

- Stay at 250-270 or drop to 200-250 for faster clears at the same monster level.
- Prioritize long paths between nodes (more monsters) over short ones.
- Avoid Azurite nodes (lower combat density).
- Delve's hidden advantage at 97.5: **no XP loss on death**. Maps cost 10% of level bar per death.
- The 97-98 grind is inherently slow at ~7% multiplier regardless of content.

## 4. Tincture — Why Boss-Only

### The Tincture

Concentrated Prismatic Tincture of Mastery:
- 45% increased Melee Weapon Attack Speed (main DPS steroid)
- 140% increased Elemental Damage with Melee Weapons
- 68% increased Mana Burn rate (stack every 0.42s — very fast)
- 35% increased effect

### Mana Burn Mechanics

- While active, you gain Mana Burn stacks over time. Each stack drains 1% max mana/sec.
- Drain is **quadratic** — after 10s at 0.42s/stack you have ~24 stacks draining 24% max mana/sec.
- Auto-deactivates at 0 mana. **8-second cooldown** on all tinctures after deactivation.

### Why It Works on Bosses

- **Boss = sustained hits = sustained mana leech.** Your 0.7% attack damage leeched as mana (Fuel the Fight + Crown of Eyes) creates leech instances per hit. Against a boss, Greater Multistrike lands constant hits, stacking leech instances that offset the burn.
- **Leech never "fills up."** Normally leech stops when mana is full. But mana burn keeps you below full, so leech instances persist (overleech effect).
- **It's a burst window.** Even on a boss, after ~15-20s the stacks outpace the 20% leech cap. You get a high-DPS burst, then deactivate and wait 8s.

### Why It Fails in Mapping

- Mobs die in 1-2 hits — only brief leech instances.
- Time spent moving between packs = zero leech but burn still ticking.
- You'd hit 0 mana, tincture deactivates, 8-second cooldown. Terrible uptime.

**TL;DR:** Activate on boss -> massive attack speed boost -> sustain mana via constant leech -> ride the window -> deactivation -> cooldown -> repeat.
